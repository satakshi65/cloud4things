Single Phase Smart Energy Meter

A BIS-approved single-phase smart energy meter is an electrical device certified by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) for accurately measuring and monitoring energy consumption in single-phase power systems. These smart meters are equipped with advanced features like remote reading, real-time monitoring, data logging, and two-way communication with the utility provider, allowing for efficient energy management and billing.

These meters are designed to enhance energy efficiency, support load management, and provide consumers with detailed insights into their energy usage. BIS certification ensures that the meters meet Indian standards for safety, performance, and reliability.

Applications:

  • Residential: Used in homes to monitor and manage household energy consumption.
  • Commercial: Suitable for small businesses or offices to track and optimize energy usage.
  • Industrial: In some cases, used in industrial settings where single-phase supply is sufficient.

Three Phase Smart Energy Meter

A BIS-approved three-phase smart energy meter is a device certified by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) for measuring and monitoring energy consumption in three-phase power systems. These meters are commonly used in industrial, commercial, and high-capacity residential settings where three-phase power is required. Below is an overview of the content and features of such meters:

Key Features:

  • High Accuracy and Reliability: BIS certification ensures compliance with Indian standards, guaranteeing high accuracy in energy measurement across all three phases.
  • Real-Time Energy Monitoring: Provides real-time data on energy consumption, voltage, current, power factor, and frequency for each phase.
  • Advanced Data Logging: Stores historical data for detailed analysis, enabling users to track consumption trends over time and optimize energy use.
  • Remote Reading and Control: Enables utility providers to read the meter remotely, reducing the need for on-site visits and improving billing accuracy.

Benefits :

  • Cost Efficiency: Accurate measurement and monitoring help in identifying areas where energy can be saved, leading to lower electricity costs.
  • Enhanced Transparency: Detailed insights into energy usage across three phases enable better management and control.
  • Operational Efficiency: Remote capabilities reduce the need for manual intervention, streamlining operations.

Smart IoT Gateway with GPRS / Ethernet

A Smart IoT Gateway with GPRS/Ethernet is a versatile device designed to connect and manage various IoT devices, enabling them to communicate with cloud-based applications or other systems over the internet. This gateway serves as a bridge between local IoT devices and remote servers, ensuring seamless data transmission, control, and monitoring.

Key Features:

  • Dual Connectivity Options: GPRS (General Packet Radio Service): Provides wireless connectivity, allowing the gateway to transmit data over cellular networks. Ideal for remote locations where wired internet is unavailable.
    Ethernet: Offers a stable and high-speed wired connection, suitable for environments with reliable network infrastructure.
  • Multi-Protocol Support: Supports various communication protocols like MQTT, CoAP, HTTP, and Modbus, enabling integration with different types of IoT devices and sensors.
  • Edge Computing Capabilities: Equipped with processing power to perform data processing, filtering, and analysis at the edge, reducing the need to send all data to the cloud.

Use Cases:

  • Smart Metering: Collects and transmits data from smart meters to utility companies for accurate billing and energy management.
  • Industrial Automation: Connects sensors and machines in industrial environments, enabling real-time monitoring, predictive maintenance, and process optimization.
  • Smart Agriculture: Gathers data from environmental sensors, such as soil moisture and weather conditions, and transmits it to cloud platforms for analysis.
  • Remote Asset Monitoring: Monitors and manages remote assets, such as oil pipelines, water tanks, and transportation fleets, ensuring operational continuity and reducing downtime.

4D Energy Meter

A 4D energy meter is an advanced type of energy meter designed to provide comprehensive and multidimensional insights into energy consumption. Unlike traditional energy meters that primarily focus on measuring electrical energy usage, a 4D energy meter offers additional layers of data and analytics, enabling users to monitor, analyze, and optimize energy consumption in a more detailed and dynamic manner.

Key Features:

  • Four-Dimensional Data Analysis: Time: Captures energy consumption over time, providing insights into peak usage periods, time-of-use patterns, and historical trends.
    Space: Monitors energy usage across different zones or locations within a facility, allowing for spatial energy management.
    Quality: Measures power quality parameters, such as voltage, current, power factor, and harmonics, ensuring optimal power supply and reducing the risk of equipment damage.
    Source: Differentiates between energy sources, such as grid power, solar, or backup generators, enabling users to manage and optimize multiple energy inputs.
  • Real-Time Monitoring and Alerts: Provides real-time data on energy consumption and power quality, allowing users to detect inefficiencies or anomalies instantly.
  • Advanced Data Logging and Analytics: Offers detailed logging of energy data, which can be analyzed to identify trends, forecast energy needs, and plan for future energy efficiency measures.

Use Cases:

  • Industrial Energy Management: Ideal for large manufacturing facilities, where energy consumption needs to be carefully managed across multiple production lines, machines, and energy sources.
  • Commercial Buildings: Used in office buildings, shopping malls, and hotels to monitor and manage energy usage across different zones, floors, and tenants.
  • Renewable Energy Integration: Essential for facilities that rely on multiple energy sources, including solar, wind, and grid power, to ensure efficient energy distribution and usage.
  • Smart Cities: Plays a critical role in smart city initiatives, where energy management needs to be coordinated across various infrastructures, such as street lighting, public buildings, and transportation systems.
